User Control and Freedom

"Users often choose system functions by mistake and will need a clearly marked "emergency exit" to leave the unwanted state without having to go through an extended dialogue. Support undo and redo." - Jakob Nielsen

Users should never feel lost when they are navigating a website. It is important to keep the user comfortable and feel like they are in control. While GSU.edu does a relatively good job of letting the user control their experience, it is easy to get lost in the website's clutter.

Inconsistency 1

When reaching the home page of the site, the user is presented with an initial navigation bar that serves as a starting point for easily navigating to different sections. The problem with this is that some of the pages that are linked to have completely different navigation bars and are for all intents and purposes in a completely different section of the site than the home page. Thus when clicking on, for example, the “Admissions” tab, it is not immediately obvious how to get back to the page from where you came because the navigation bar headings have changed.

Universal Navigation

Fortunately, the site offers a simple solution to this problem. Any time a user leaves the home section, a small dropdown menu entitled “Georgia State Home” is shown on the top left of each page. This dropdown is essentially the exact same navigation bar shown on the homepage of the site in dropdown form, and serves to guide the user to wherever they want to go at any time, or back to where they were before, assuming they remember how they arrived.

Inconsistency 2

Unfortunately, the dropdown/home navigation bar links to some pages that do not include that universal dropdown. Clicking on either the “Alumni” or “Athletics” pages, and many of the home pages of the University's Colleges essentially lead the user to completely different websites that bear no resemblance or link to other parts of the website.
