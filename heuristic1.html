Visibility of System Status

"The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time." - Jakob Nielsen

It is important for users of a website to be able to easily locate where or how deep they are in the site. This helps make the user be more comfortable with navigating around the site with ease. GSU.edu adheres to this design aspect well.

Title/Header

The site is basically divided up into a top-level main section with general information about the school, and into separate subsections for each individual department such as research, athletics, etc, as well as home pages for individual colleges. The top left of each page of the site is clearly marked with the appropriate title/heading to let the user know where they are/which department's page they are in, and what kind of content they are viewing.

Navigation Bar Highlight

Each separate subsection of the site also has its own navigation bar that highlights which part of the particular subsection the user is currently on. For example, for the University's Research section, the appropriate heading of the navigation bar at the top of the page is underlined red to let the user know whether they are on the "About" page, "Facilities" page, "Resources" page, etc. This style of top-level navigation is, for the most part, consistent throughout each part of the site.

Secondary Navigation

In addition to the top navigation bar, certain subpages of each category of the navigation bar also include a side navigation interface that shows how deep the user is into the page hierarchy of that particular category. Coming back to the University's Research section, if one were to hover over the "Centers and Institutes" button on the top navigation bar and click on the "About Us" link in the dropdown, the resulting page includes a grey side navigator that not only lets you know what part of the "Centers and Institutes" section you are in, but also lets you easily access every other part of that section. Unfortunately, however, this side navigation does not always show up, leaving the user to look to the title heading or navigation bar to see where they are.
