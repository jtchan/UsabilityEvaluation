Nielsen's 10 Heuristics

"The 10 most general principles for interaction design. They are called "heuristics" because they are more in the nature of rules of thumb than specific usability guidelines." - Jakob Nielsen

Our goal was to conduct a thorough usability evaluation on the website of Georgia State University. We followed the guidelines provided by Jakob Nielsen in his 10 Heuristics. Using these heuristics, we found both positive and negative design choices in the top few layers of the site. While assessing the site, we kept in mind that design should always place the user first and foremost. After our evaluation, we came away with a better understanding of what makes efficient versus inefficient design. Our findings are recorded on the pages to follow. Enjoy our usability evaluation!
