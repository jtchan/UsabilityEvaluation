Match Between System and the Real World

"The system should speak the users' language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order." - Jakob Nielsen

A website should use clear, understandble, and unambiguous language that is geared to its userbase.

Common Terms

The language of the site is for the most part unambiguous and appropriate for its target audience. "Academics", "Admissions", "Research", etc., are all common terms that can be understood by prospective students, professors, interested parents, or any others that have a reason to visit the University's website. These links take the user to the appropriate pages, where the user can find out more information about the topic at hand.

Ambiguity

One of the links in the second navigation bar is titled “Giving”. This is a somewhat vague term because it can imply several different ideas. “Giving” can mean the university’s philanthropic acts to the community or ways that the community has given back to the university. However, the page that the link takes the user to is actually about donations that have been given to the university, and not things that the university has given back to the community.

Acronyms

Additionally, there are many acronyms in both the navigation bars at the top of the page. Although these acronyms may be easily understood by students and faculty associated with the university, they are very confusing to those outside of the community. Even when the user clicks on the link, the page might contain login information, which provides no explanation about the page to the unaffiliated user. For example, one of the links “GoSOLAR” takes the user to a page where current students can take care of student account business, such as registration and immunization records. However, from the name of the link itself, it is impossible to know what the page is about.
